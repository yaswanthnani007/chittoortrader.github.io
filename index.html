The Book

RoadMap to Trading Success

A personal account of ego, revenge, fear, patience and control — and how each one, in its turn, rewrote the way I trade.

I have started my trading career during 2020 COVID outbreak with the thought of buying stocks for low price. Which laid the foundation to enter into trading arena. I have entered with the wrong thinking that "Let's give it a try", instead of taking it a serious profession where even the smartest minds struggle to keep money which was made. I hoped to become financially independent soon. Which slowly hit me with a hammer to wake up. By the time I realised I was in debt with a loan I was struggling repay.

A strategy can get you into a good trade. Only your psychology decides whether you actually follow it, whether you cut the loss you said you'd cut, and whether you take the profit you planned to take instead of hoping for more.

Almost nobody blows up an account because their setup was wrong. They blow up because fear closed a winner too early, greed held a loser too long, or revenge trading tried to claw back in an hour what took a month to build.

Strategy tells you what to do. Psychology decides if you'll actually do it.

01

Why Trade?

Every trader has an origin story, and mine begins in a locked-down house in 2020, watching prices fall and thinking it looked like an opportunity rather than a warning. Cheap stocks felt like a doorway to something better — faster, easier than the slow climb of a job. Nobody tells you at the start that trading isn't a bet you place once and collect on; it's a profession that quietly tests your character every single day, and it doesn't care how badly you need it to work.

02

The Ego

The account grew, and so did the certainty that I'd figured something out that other people hadn't. That's the trap — a few good trades feel like proof of skill, when they're often just proof of luck wearing skill's clothes. Somewhere in there, being right stopped being about the money and started being about me. I wasn't managing risk anymore. I was defending an identity.

03

The Revenge

When the market finally proved the ego wrong, I didn't step back — I stepped in harder. Bigger size, faster entries, no plan except the desperate need to get back what I'd just lost, right now. Revenge trading isn't a strategy; it's grief wearing a trading terminal, trying to undo the last hour instead of accepting it. It rarely gets the money back. It usually just adds to what's owed.

The best revenge is your success.

04

The Fear

After enough of those spirals, the fear arrived — not as caution, but as paralysis. I started hesitating on setups I would once have taken without a second thought, cutting winners early out of a fear they'd turn into losses, and flinching at every red candle like it was aimed at me personally. Fear felt like the opposite of the recklessness before it, but it came from the exact same place: a nervous system that no longer trusted itself.

05

The Patience

Patience turned out to be the hardest skill in trading, because it isn't passive — it's the active discipline of doing nothing while everything in you wants to act. Waiting for the right setup instead of forcing a mediocre one. Sitting through boredom instead of manufacturing a trade to feel busy. I had to learn that the market rewards those willing to wait longer than it rewards those willing to work harder.

Patience is your deadliest weapon.

06

The Control

I spent years trying to control the market — predicting it, outsmarting it, willing it to move my way. None of that was ever within reach. What was within reach, the whole time, was smaller and far more useful: my position size, my exit, my reaction to being wrong. The day I stopped trying to control the market and started controlling myself was the day the account stopped bleeding.

07

Why Rules?

A rule is just a decision made by the calmer version of me, left behind as instructions for the version who won't be calm later. Every chapter before this one is a story about what happens when there's nothing standing between an emotion and a trade. Rules are that thing. Not a cage — a rope handed to a future self who's about to be underwater.

The Principles

The Rules That Keep Me Building

A rule isn't a cage. It's the version of me that's thinking clearly, leaving instructions for the version of me that won't be — the one who's down bad, wide awake at 2 a.m., staring at a red candle like it's staring back.

01

Rules protect me from myself, not from the market

The market was never the real risk. My reaction to it was.

02

Decide the exit before the entry

A stop-loss written in advance is a decision. A stop-loss decided mid-trade is a hope.

03

Position size the risk, not the conviction

Certainty is a feeling. Risk is a number. Trade the number.

04

One setup, followed, beats ten opinions, argued

A plan I trust beats a hunch I can't explain by morning.

05

Every trade gets logged, not judged

A file entry tells the truth. A verdict just makes me feel something about it.

06

Consistency compounds. Certainty never arrives

I don't need to know what happens next. I need a process that survives not knowing.

About the Author

Yashwanth G

Yashwanth G is a trader based in Chittoor, India, and the writer behind Chittoor Trader. A software engineer by training, he began trading Indian equities and forex in 2020 and spent years learning — the hard way — that psychology, not strategy, decides most outcomes in the market. RoadMap to Trading Success is his account of that education: the ego, the losses, the debt, and the rules he built to make sure the comeback stuck.
